Our Game Birds

We have many different game birds. Most must live in a closed aviary to avoid losing them. While they are not as endearing as chickens, they are stunning.

Pheasants

Red Golden Pheasant

The red golden pheasant is one of the most beautiful and colored breeds of pheasants. Male pheasant feathers are bright red for their body and bright yellow for their head. They also have an impressive long tail. Females are mostly brown with different shades and a shorter tail.

Pros:

  • Colorful

Cons:

  • Need a closed aviary
  • Fearful and wild

Yellow Golden Pheasant

The yellow golden pheasant is a variant of the red golden pheasant. Instead of having red feathers, male pheasants have yellow feathers on their bodies. Females are also paler than red golden females. They are a shade of beige and tan. Currently, we only have females on our farm.

Pros:

  • Colorful

Cons:

  • Need a closed aviary
  • Fearful and wild

Ring-necked Pheasant

The ring-necked pheasant is the common wild pheasant in North America. Male bodies are of different shades of brown, beige, gray, and black. At their neck, they have a white band, hence their name. The feathers on their heads are dark blue and dark green. They also have large red wattles. We have one male at our farm.

Pros:

  • Best breed for meat

Cons:

  • Need a closed aviary
  • Fearful and wild

Partridges

Chukar Partridge

The Chakar partridge is among the most common breeds raised on hobby farms. Its feathers are mostly grey, with black and white stripes on the wings. Males and females have similar feathers. They love to roost. We have two partridges on our farm.

Pros:

  • Best breed for meat

Cons:

  • Need a closed aviary
  • Fearful and wild

Guinea Fowl

White Guinea Fowl

Guinea fowl are one of the most intriguing game birds. The white variety has white feathers and no feathers on the head. They also have a small bump on their head. They love searching for bugs. If they are used to it when they are young, guinea fowl can live with chicken. However, they fly high and will probably escape a few times. Our guinea fowl got her name Pommette (the French word for crabapple) when she decided to roost at the highest branch of our apple tree during a cold winter night.

Pros:

  • Eat undesirable bugs
  • Great for meat production
  • Live with chickens

Cons:

  • Will escape and roost on nearby trees or structures
  • Noisy
  • Fearful and wild but less than pheasants
