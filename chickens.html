Our Chicken Breeds

We have many different breeds of chickens on our farm. The following list presents the breeds with the highest number of chickens. We also discuss the advantages and disadvantages of owning each breed.

Polish

Polish chickens are probably one of the most styled breeds of chicken. The feathers on top of their heads give them a unique look. They are incredibly kind, lovable, and calm. However, from our experience, they arenâ€™t the most intelligent breeds. Do not be surprised to find them lost far from the flock.

Pros:

  • Calm and kind
  • Unique look
  • Multiple color varieties

Cons:

  • Small egg
  • Medium egg production
  • It is best if they live in an enclosure since they get lost easily

Silkie

Silkies are one of the most unique chicken breeds. Their feathers are fluffy and soft. They are also one of the most docile breeds of chicken. Silkies are exceptional mothers and are always broody.

Pros:

  • Perfect for raising chicks
  • Extremely calm and docile
  • Unique look

Cons:

  • Smaller eggs
  • Lower egg production since hen are often broody
  • Vulnerabre against predator since they do not fly

Plymouth

Plymouth is one of the most popular backyard chicken breeds. It is easy and an excellent choice for any beginner. They lay big brown eggs regularly, making it one of the best breeds for eggs.

Pros:

  • Perfect for beginners
  • Perfect for egg production
  • Calm and docile

Cons:

  • Less unique

Barbu d'Anvers

These chickens might be small, but they are not afraid. They will pass through small holes or fly over fences to explore outside their enclosure. At our farm, our hens are incredibly kind and loving. However, our rooster, Violet, is aggressive and will do anything to protect his hens.

Pros:

  • They could live in a smaller coop since they are small
  • Rarely broody

Cons:

  • They are escape artists
  • Roosters can be aggressive

Light Sussex

The Light Sussex breed is one of the most popular. It is highly similar to the Plymouth breed and lays big eggs regularly. It is also a good choice for beginners. Overall, they are easy to care for, produce many large eggs, and are calm and docile.

Pros:

  • Perfect for beginners
  • Perfect for egg production
  • Calm and docile

Cons:

  • Less unique
