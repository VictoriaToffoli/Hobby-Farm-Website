Our pens

One of the most important things to consider when owning farm animals is their pens. We must offer them a home to protect them from predators and weather. Their home must also cover their essential needs. The following will present our animal home; we hope it inspires you while creating yours.

The barn

The barn is our main chicken home. They always have access to it. It is an old barn that we renovated. One of the additions was to insulate. We also added electricity and did some sections. We slowly renovate some parts each year. We use wood chopping for the bedding since we find cleaning easier. We also offer them nesting boxes and roosting bars. We put a toddler pool filled with sand in winter so the chickens can take dust baths. We also have a separate section inaccessible to animals, with an old wood stove that we often use during the winter to heat and reduce humidity. We try to keep the temperature close to freezing during the winter.

Pros:

  • It is possible to have electricity.
  • It can be the home of multiple animals (birds, rabbits, goats, and others).
  • It can have multiple sections.

Cons:

  • It must have ventilation.
  • It is larger, making it more difficult to heat in winter.
  • It is prone to rats and mice.

The aviary

The aviary is the home of our pheasants. We separated the aviary into two sections. The first is for our pheasants, and the second is the space where chickens come during winter if they want some sun. We added boxes for the pheasant to make nests and many high roosting bars. The chicken section is connected to the barn by a tunnel, and we can close it at night. During winter, we place large transparent tarpaulin that isolates the aviary. We remove them gradually when the temperature rises. The metal fences of the aviary are also buried into the ground so predators can not dig to pass under the walls.

Pros:

  • It protects chickens from predators.
  • It protects birds from the sun, rain, and snow.
  • It is perfect for game birds.

Cons:

  • It takes time to build it.
  • It is impossible to move.
  • Grass or vegetation will not survive long.

The courtyard

Chickens, ducks, and geese can have fun in the courtyard during summer. The yard has multiple trees, sand, grass, and a small pond. A long electric fence surrounds the yard so the animals will not wander too far. The electric fence also protects our flock from dogs and other animals.

Pros:

  • Electric fences are perfect for delimiting a zone.
  • Birds can have access to natural elements like grass.

Cons:

  • Some chickens and ducks can escape.
  • Electric fences are great but not failproof.
  • Birds still need a coop to protect them from the weather.

The chicken tractor

The chicken tractor is popular for those who raise meat chicken. The wheel behind it allows the tractor to be moved easily, allowing meat chicken to have fresh grass daily. Chicken tractors are also great for other purposes. Our chicken used one to hang out, sheltered from the sun and rain, before we had the aviary. They are also a great place to isolate new chickens for a few days.

Pros:

  • It is perfect for meat chicken.
  • It is great for isolating new chickens.
  • It can be easily moved.

Cons:

  • It offers limited space.
  • Predators can enter as there is no wire on the bottom.

The waterfowl winter house

The waterfowl winter house is where the ducks and geese pass winter. They have a large portable garage with a small courtyard delimited by wooden fences. We put a thick layer of hay under the portable garage to cut the cold. We closed the garage door each night to protect the birds from the cold. During the day, we opened the door, and they could use the small courtyard. Our breed of ducks and geese are extremely cold-resistant; they pass through the cold winter without any problem.

Pros:

  • It is easy to build and replace.
  • Ducks and geese did not make a mess in the barn.

Cons:

  • We need to replace the water regularly since it freezes fast.
